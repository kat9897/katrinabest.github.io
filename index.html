About Me

Katrina is a third year statistics specialist with co-op and psychology major who is currently pursuing her computer science passion. She is a first year peer advancing her technological skills and programming languages through extracurricular projects and jobs. Her self-motivation drove her to compete in several different competitions such as the Makerspace Stop Motion Film Festival, The Hub's Entrepreneurship Pitch Competition, UTSC's Undergraduate Forum Competition, and others. In her free time, she loves to cook and continues to practice karate as a black belt and enhance her self defense knowledge.

Passionate

Katrina's drive for programming enables her to seek out diverse opportunities and continue to learn as both a student and in her free time.

Hardworking

When Katrina sets her mind to a task, she doesn't rest until it's done. During Hack the Valley 4 in February 2020, she stayed up for approximately 40 hours to finish her project Seasoning Shack.

Problem Solving

During her vast coding endeavours, Katrina has learned that it doesn't matter what languages she knows, but her ability to learn new technologies quickly. She demonstrates this through several of her projects and jobs, including Pet Sprout, her Software Developer Intern position at TshirtGang, The Invisible Cart, and more.
